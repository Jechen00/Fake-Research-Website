The Anatomy of the Shrinking Clown

A few years being relieved of my clown duties, I was given the opportunity to join a research group focused on understanding the complex anatomical structure of a clown in 2013. Specifically, the research group was interested in learning about the components that contribute to a clowns trademark ability of being able to fit into a small car already occupied by 30 plus people. With dissections and post-mortem examinations on clowns apparently being ruled unconstitutional by the Supreme Court (thankfully), the research group resorted to a set of oral testimonies and "peaceful" trials, in which they were able to observe how different body parts contorted and interacted with one another to allow the clown to effectively shrink in size. As an ex-clown, my role was primarily centered on vitial community outreach and guiding the research group through the ironically unfunny history of clown culture, as we used this traditional knowledge, in conjunction with the data, to draw a coherent conclusion.
